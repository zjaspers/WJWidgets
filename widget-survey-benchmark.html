Survey Design
Keep Surveys to 3-5 Questions
7-Eleven's surveys are brief. Long surveys (10+ questions) kill response rates. Ask one thing well instead of everything poorly. Target: <2 minutes to complete.
Timing Strategy
Launch Surveys During Shift, Not After
Launch surveys Tuesday-Thursday, 10am-2pm. Employees on the clock = higher completion. Off-hours surveys get ignored. Make it part of the workday.
Follow-Through
Close the Loop: "You Said, We Did"
If 70% of your team says break room needs updating, post an update within 2 weeks. "You told us the break room needs help. We ordered new furniture—arriving Friday." Trust = future responses.
Manager Role
Discuss Results in Team Huddles
Monday huddle: "Last week's survey showed 80% feel supported. That's great! 20% don't—let's talk about why." Make surveys conversational, not corporate.
Accountability
Add Survey Completion % to Store Scorecard
Track "Survey Response Rate" alongside sales and shrink. Target: 30%+ response rate. Celebrate stores that hit it. Coach stores below 20%.
